Panther Coders:
Locks of
Love

“Our mission is to return a sense of self, confidence and normalcy to children suffering from hair loss by utilizing donated ponytails to provide the highest quality hair prosthetics to financially disadvantaged children free of charge.”

Locks of Love was started in 1997, spearheaded by Madonna Coffman who not only lost her hair, but had to watch her daughter go through the same ordeal. Donations were originally stored in her garage before a local hospital donated office space.

Locks of Love makes hair prosthetics for financially disadvantaged children ages 21 and under who have lost their hair. Their goal is to help all children suffering from hair loss to recover and so far they have succeeded by helping thousands of children.

If you want to donate your hair, money, or time, Locks of Love could use your contribution. You can donate at their website,Locksoflove.org
